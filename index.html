Wave Anatomy

Elements 1–5
1
Impulse Wave
Core Easy
Sustained directional move with momentum

A strong directional price move where buyers (or sellers) overwhelm the opposite side. Candles are large-bodied, consecutive, and in the same direction. Volume is above average. Pullbacks are small or absent.

In Your Strategy

Option buying works ONLY during impulse waves — premiums expand fast, theta is irrelevant. Your rule 5.2/5.4 (gap + 15-min + 5-min confirmation) is designed to catch the start of an impulse. Outside impulses, theta kills you.

2
Corrective Wave
Core Easy
Counter-trend pullback between impulses

A pullback or pause against the prevailing trend. Smaller candles, overlapping ranges, lower volume than the impulse. Corrections retrace a portion (typically 38–62%) of the prior impulse before the trend resumes.

In Your Strategy

NEVER buy options during a correction — theta + IV crush + sideways price = guaranteed loss. Your "no trade between EMAs" rule (5.6) and "no trade 11:30–14:00" rule (3.3) exist because that's when corrections dominate. Wait for the impulse.

3
Wave Amplitude
Core Medium
Vertical distance from start to end of wave

The vertical distance (in points) covered by a wave from start to peak. Bigger amplitude = stronger conviction. Compare the amplitude of consecutive waves — when impulses shrink and corrections grow, trend is weakening.

In Your Strategy

Your minimum 60-point Nifty target is essentially a minimum amplitude requirement. If the setup's projected amplitude is < 60 points (price near resistance, tight range, low VIX), skip it. Strong amplitude days = trail SL and let it run.

4
Wave Symmetry
Important Medium
Equal time + equal distance = symmetry

A wave is symmetric when its time duration and price distance match a prior wave. Markets often produce "measured moves" where Wave B equals Wave A. The 1:1 projection is the most reliable target for the next leg.

In Your Strategy

After a 60-point Wave A move, project the same 60 points for Wave B as your target. If a CHoCH happens and Wave A was 80 points, expect Wave B to extend at least 80 points before booking. Use prior wave size to calculate trail-SL distance.

5
Swing Points (HH HL LH LL)
Core Medium
Higher Highs/Lows = uptrend · Lower Highs/Lows = downtrend

The four building blocks of market structure. HH = price makes a higher high than the prior peak. HL = pullback ends above the prior trough. Sequence of HH+HL = uptrend. Sequence of LH+LL = downtrend. Mixed = transition.

In Your Strategy

Replaces subjective "bullish/bearish" classification in your rule 2.1. Check the last 3 swing points on the 1-day chart. HH+HL → bias is bullish, only take CE setups. LH+LL → bias is bearish, only take PE setups. Mixed → wait.

Elliott Wave Structure

Elements 6–10
6
5-Wave Impulse
Core Medium
Three up-legs separated by two pullbacks

The classic Elliott structure for a trending move. Waves 1, 3, 5 are impulses in the trend direction. Waves 2 and 4 are corrections against it. Wave 3 is usually the longest. Wave 4 cannot overlap Wave 1's territory.

In Your Strategy

Identify which wave you're in BEFORE entering. Best CE entries: start of Wave 3 (after Wave 2 finds support at EMA). Avoid entering at Wave 5 — exhaustion is near. Wave 3 is where your "60-point minimum" target almost always works.

7
3-Wave Correction (ABC)
Core Medium
A-B-C: two impulses against, one with

After a 5-wave impulse completes, price corrects in 3 waves: A (against trend), B (a bounce that fools traders into re-entering), C (final move against trend). Wave C often equals Wave A in length — measured move principle.

In Your Strategy

Wave B is the trap. Most retail buys here thinking the trend resumed — then C wipes them out. If you see a rally after a 5-wave move, DO NOT enter CE — it might be Wave B. Wait for Wave C to complete, then take the next 5-wave structure.

8
Wave 1 — Initiation
Important Hard
First impulse after trend exhaustion

The first push in a new trend, born from disbelief. Most traders are still positioned in the old trend and dismiss Wave 1 as a "bounce." Volume is moderate. Wave 1 often coincides with a CHoCH on the higher timeframe.

In Your Strategy

Wave 1 is risky to trade because confirmation is weak — you might be catching a Wave B of a continuing correction. Better play: let Wave 1 complete, wait for Wave 2 pullback, then enter at Wave 3 start. That's your rule 5.5 gap-down reversal trade.

9
Wave 3 — Extension
Core Medium
The strongest and most tradable wave

Wave 3 is the most powerful wave — fastest, steepest, with the highest volume. By Elliott's rule, Wave 3 is NEVER the shortest of waves 1, 3, 5. Retail traders finally believe the trend and pile in, fueling the move. This is where most of the profit lives.

In Your Strategy

This IS the trade. Your 15-min + 5-min confirmation, entering at the EMA reclaim or break — you're entering at the start of Wave 3. Trail SL aggressively. Wave 3 days are when the 60-point target gets blown past and you should let it run to 150+ points.

10
Wave 5 — Exhaustion
Important Hard
Final push before correction — exit zone

The last wave of an impulse. Often shorter than Wave 3, on lower volume, with RSI divergence. Wave 5 frequently makes a new high/low but momentum is fading. The smart money is distributing here while retail is buying euphorically.

In Your Strategy

NEVER initiate new options here — you're buying the top. If you're already in a profitable trade from Wave 3, Wave 5 is your EXIT signal. Tighten trail SL to half the prior wave's range. Then watch for the ABC correction to set up the next opposite trade.

SMC Wave Concepts

Elements 11–15
11
Liquidity Sweep Wave
Core Medium
Stop hunt below support, then violent reversal

A wave that briefly pushes below an obvious support (or above resistance) to trigger clustered stop-losses, then violently reverses. The wick is long, the close is back inside the prior range. This is operators harvesting liquidity to fill their own orders.

In Your Strategy

Today's Nifty chart is exactly this — long-wick green candle at the bottom. Entry trigger: sweep + close back inside + bullish 5-min confirmation = CE buy. SL goes BELOW the sweep wick, not above it. This is rule 5.5 with maximum confluence.

12
Break of Structure (BOS)
Core Medium
Body close past prior swing point

A candle whose body closes beyond the most recent swing high (bullish BOS) or low (bearish BOS). Wicks alone don't count. BOS confirms that the prevailing trend has institutional backing and is likely to continue.

In Your Strategy

Your filter for false EMA breakouts in rule 5.7. When the 5-min closes above an EMA, also check: does it close above the last swing high? Yes = real breakout, enter. No = suspect, wait for one more confirmation candle. BOS is your "trend is real" signal.

13
Change of Character (CHoCH)
Important Hard
First close beyond prior opposing swing

The first candle in a downtrend to close above the most recent Lower High (or in an uptrend, below the last Higher Low). Unlike BOS which confirms continuation, CHoCH is the earliest warning that the trend may have flipped. It precedes BOS.

In Your Strategy

The signal for your rule 5.5 (gap-down reversal). If price gaps down, builds LH+LL structure, then closes above the last LH — that's a CHoCH and your highest-confidence reversal trade. Combine with sweep + bullish 5-min candle for maximum edge.

14
Internal vs External Structure
Advanced Hard
Small waves nest inside larger waves

A larger wave is made of smaller waves. The external structure is the major trend visible on higher timeframes. Internal structure is what's happening within each external wave on lower timeframes. They can be opposite — internal correction within external impulse.

In Your Strategy

Your 1-day external structure (rule 2.1) sets directional bias. Your 5-min internal structure provides entry. Best trades = both align. If 1-day is bullish (external) and 5-min just printed a CHoCH up (internal flip), that's the highest-edge setup.

15
Wave Confluence with Zones
Advanced Medium
Wave terminates exactly at a demand zone

When a wave's endpoint coincides with a supply/demand zone, a previous day level, or an EMA, the probability of reversal multiplies. Single signal = decent. Two-way confluence = strong. Three or more = highest-edge setup.

In Your Strategy

Use this for entry quality scoring. Wave ends + 200 EMA + PDL + OI Put-writing wall all within 30 points of each other = score 4 setup. Take full size, trail SL aggressively. No confluence = skip, even if all your standard rules tick.

Wave Patterns

Elements 16–20
16
Zigzag Correction
Core Easy
Sharp ABC where C exceeds A

The most aggressive corrective pattern. Wave A is a 5-wave impulse against trend. Wave B is a shallow 3-wave bounce that rarely exceeds 61.8% of A. Wave C is another 5-wave impulse, equal to or longer than A. Total move is steep and fast.

In Your Strategy

Recognize zigzags as continuation patterns within larger trends. After a 5-wave move up + sharp zigzag down + price holds above prior swing low = the bigger trend resumes. Take CE on the Wave C completion. Use Wave A's length to project where C will end.

17
Flat Correction
Core Easy
Sideways ABC where B nearly equals A

A sideways correction. Wave A is a weak 3-wave move. Wave B retraces back almost to where A started — fooling traders into thinking the trend resumed. Wave C is a 5-wave move that ends near or just past A. Total range is contained within a horizontal box.

In Your Strategy

Flats are typical of 11:30–14:00 sideways chop (your no-trade zone). DO NOT trade through them. Wait for the breakout from the flat's range — that breakout is usually Wave 3 of the larger trend. Entry on the breakout candle's 5-min confirmation.

18
Triangle Consolidation
Important Medium
Converging price action → expansion

Five waves of decreasing amplitude — lower highs and higher lows converging to a point. Volume contracts. Tension builds. The breakout direction (usually the direction of the trend before the triangle) starts a powerful new impulse.

In Your Strategy

Triangles often form during your 11:30–14:00 no-trade zone. Mark the triangle's apex. The 2:00 PM conditional window (rule 3.4) is perfect for trading triangle breakouts — explosive moves into the close. Entry on 5-min body close beyond the trendline.

19
Diagonal Wedge
Advanced Hard
Rising wedge ends in violent reversal

A wedge has converging trendlines like a triangle, but both lines slope in the same direction. Rising wedge = both lines slope up, but the slope is decreasing — bearish reversal pattern. Falling wedge = bullish reversal. Wedges typically appear at Wave 5.

In Your Strategy

If you spot a rising wedge forming on the 5-min during a CE position — book profits immediately, do not wait for the break. Wedges resolve violently. After confirmation (5-min close below the lower trendline), consider a PE entry per rule 5.7 on the opposite side.

20
Truncated Wave 5
Advanced Hard
Wave 5 fails to exceed Wave 3 high

When Wave 5 fails to break above the Wave 3 peak (in an uptrend), the impulse has "truncated." This is a strong signal of trend exhaustion and predicts a sharp reversal. The market literally couldn't muster the strength for a new high.

In Your Strategy

Truncation is your "take profit NOW" signal. If you're in a CE position and price tries to make a new high but stalls and rolls over below the prior peak — exit immediately. Don't wait for SL hit. The subsequent move down is often as long as the entire 1-3 advance.

Trading Application

Elements 21–24
21
Wave 2 Entry — Pullback Buy
Core Medium
Entry on Wave 2 completion at 50-62% retrace

After Wave 1 completes, price pulls back in Wave 2 — typically retracing 50-62% of Wave 1. Wave 2 commonly ends at the 20 EMA or a demand zone. Entry here gives the best risk-to-reward because Wave 3 is about to begin.

In Your Strategy

This is your highest R:R entry. After morning impulse + pullback to 20 EMA + 5-min bullish confirmation = Wave 2 complete, Wave 3 starting. SL goes below the Wave 2 low (small SL = ₹10k risk easy to manage). Trail aggressively as Wave 3 unfolds.

22
Wave 3 Continuation Entry
Core Easy
Entry on break above Wave 1 high — BOS confirms Wave 3

A more conservative entry than Wave 2. Wait for price to break above the Wave 1 high (a BOS) — this confirms Wave 3 has started. You give up some R:R for confirmation. Reward: lowest chance of being wrong about whether the pullback is over.

In Your Strategy

This matches your strict rule-based entry style. After morning Wave 1 + pullback + price breaks the morning high with 5-min body close + volume spike = enter CE. SL goes below the pullback low. Target the prior impulse's 1.618x extension.

23
Wave 5 Exhaustion Exit
Important Medium
Exit at Wave 5 — don't ride the reversal

If you entered at Wave 2 or Wave 3, the goal is to exit at Wave 5's peak, not ride through the correction. Signs Wave 5 is ending: divergence on RSI/MACD, volume declining as price rises, candles getting smaller, approach to a major resistance/zone.

In Your Strategy

Don't get greedy. Once 60-point target hit + Wave 5 signs appearing (volume dropping, smaller candles, approach to 200 EMA from below) = exit. Your strategy already says "exit at 60 points on slow days" — Wave 5 exhaustion is when that rule must override the "trail" rule.

24
Wave C Reversal Entry
Advanced Hard
Buy at Wave C completion — catch the new impulse

When an ABC correction completes, the original trend resumes with a fresh 5-wave impulse. The C wave's end is the highest-edge entry point — you're entering at the start of a new larger trend. Wave C often equals Wave A (measured move).

In Your Strategy

The textbook gap-down reversal trade (rule 5.5). Morning gap down = could be Wave A or C of a larger correction. If price sweeps liquidity + prints bullish 5-min + CHoCH back above prior LH = Wave C complete, new trend starting. Take CE with full conviction.
